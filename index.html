Effects of Performing Augmented Reality Tasks on Postural Sway While on a Ladder

Designed a study in which participants completed four simulated ladder-based work tasks with AR assistance, examining an interaction — AR use during elevated manual work — with no prior formal research. Data collected and analyzed in MATLAB and JMP (Full thesis only available upon request due to pending publication).

Key finding

Interacting with AR interfaces on a ladder increases postural sway (i.e., worsens balance) compared to passive tasks like reading, and this effect is worst for interactive tasks (like button-poking) using a world-relative (environment-fixed) display, meaning AR interface design for construction workers on ladders should favor screen-relative displays for interaction-heavy tasks to reduce fall risk.
